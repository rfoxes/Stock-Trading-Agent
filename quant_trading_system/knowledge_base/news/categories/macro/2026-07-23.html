Jobless claims fell to 187,000 — the fewest since 1969.
AP / US News / Washington Times · 2026-07-23
Initial unemployment claims for the week ending 7/18 dropped 22,000 to 187,000, well below the ~215,000 consensus and the lowest weekly reading since September 1969. The 4-week average fell to 207,500. A very strong labor-market print — but combined with the oil spike it pushed yields and inflation expectations higher rather than reading as an unambiguous positive.
10-year Treasury yield rose to its highest since January 2025.
CNBC · 2026-07-23
The 10-year yield climbed to an ~18-month high (~4.6%+) as surging oil (Brent >$100) rekindled inflation fear and the 1969-low jobless print removed any near-term easing case. Higher long rates are a headwind for growth multiples and rate-sensitive flows into the 7/28–29 FOMC.
Oil topped $100 — the dominant macro force of the session.
Bloomberg / The National / CNBC · 2026-07-23
Brent crude rallied +6.45% to ~$101.10/bbl — above $100 for the first time since May and up ~40% month-to-date — after Iran-backed Houthis struck two Saudi oil tankers (Encelia, Layla) in the Red Sea (see geopolitics). Goldman flagged Brent could reach $120+ by Q4 if disruptions persist. The fresh inflationary tail into the FOMC.
Equity tape: a genuine risk-off session — but orderly, not a break.
Washington Post wire / CNBC · 2026-07-23
S&P 500 −1.21% (7,408.30), Nasdaq Composite −2.15% (25,137.69), Dow −0.97% (51,711.65). The decline was concentrated in two mega-cap earnings reactions — Alphabet (~−7%) and Tesla (~−14%) — plus $100 oil; the Roundhill Magnificent Seven ETF fell ~4%, its worst session since April 2025. Notably VIX eased to 16.64 (see volatility): an idiosyncratic, earnings-driven pullback, not a systemic fear event. No first-tier data surprise beyond the jobless print; next FOMC 7/28–29.
