June PPI cooled sharply — well below consensus (released 7/15, 8:30 AM ET)
CNBC / Bloomberg / Seeking Alpha · 2026-07-15
Producer prices fell -0.3% MoM (vs -0.1% consensus; a sharp reversal from May's +0.6%) → 5.5% YoY (vs 6.2% consensus, down from a revised 6.0%). Core PPI +0.2% MoM (vs +0.4% expected) → 4.7% YoY (vs 5.2%). Goods -1.4% MoM (biggest drop since July 2022), gasoline -12% (about two-thirds of the monthly decline). Second cool inflation print in two days (after June CPI 7/14); reinforces a Fed-on-hold expectation and cut July rate-hike odds. YoY level still elevated (5.5%). Last major inflation reads before the 7/28–29 FOMC. Caveat: June data predates the current Iran-driven oil spike.
Empire State (NY Fed) manufacturing jumped in July — beat
NY Fed / Investing.com · 2026-07-15
General business conditions index 15.6 vs 8.8 expected and 5.7 in June (+10 pts). New orders +19 to 22.2; shipments +16 to 24.4 (four-year high); employment index 11.4 (highest since Dec 2022). Supply availability stayed negative (-10.0). A solid signal on the real economy alongside the cool inflation prints.
Fed Chair Warsh — Senate Banking testimony (second day of semiannual testimony)
CNBC / Washington Post / Xinhua · 2026-07-15
Hawkish-consistent with his 7/14 House debut: pledged to make five years of high inflation "a thing of the past," "no tolerance for persistently elevated inflation," and "we need a regime change in policy" (launched five internal task forces on communications, balance sheet, data, productivity/jobs, and inflation frameworks). Gave few clues on rate direction; sidestepped Senate questions on AI's inflation impact and his contacts with President Trump. Net: keeps a hawkish framing on top of the two cool prints. Fed funds held 3.50–3.75% at the June meeting.
