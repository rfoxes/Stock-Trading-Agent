June CPI came in COOLER than expected on both headline and core — the biggest monthly headline drop since April 2020.
BLS / CNBC / CBS · 2026-07-14 (8:30 AM ET release)
Headline CPI -0.4% MoM (vs -0.2% consensus), pulling the annual rate to 3.5% YoY (vs 3.8% expected, down from 4.2% in May) — the largest monthly decline since April 2020. Core CPI (ex-food/energy) was FLAT MoM (vs +0.2% expected), 12-month rate 2.6% YoY (vs 2.9% expected, down from 2.9%). The move was energy-led: the energy index slumped -5.7% MoM (biggest drop since April 2020) though still +15.7% YoY (gasoline +26.7% YoY). This is the last inflation read before the 7/28-29 FOMC and it slashed the odds of a near-term Fed rate hike. NOTE: this June print predates the current oil spike from the US-Iran/Hormuz escalation (see geopolitics), so it does not yet capture the energy re-acceleration now underway.
Fed Chair Warsh's first semi-annual congressional testimony: hawkish-measured — "no tolerance" for inflation, rejects "mission accomplished."
Federal Reserve / CNN / Spectrum News · 2026-07-14 (House Financial Services)
In his congressional debut, Warsh said the Fed has "no tolerance for persistently elevated inflation" and a "resolute commitment to restoring price stability." On the same-morning cool June CPI he pushed back on any dovish read: "There might be some that look at this morning's data and say 'mission accomplished' — that is not my view," calling it one month of data. Consistent with his stated low-guidance style, he did not signal whether hikes would be needed. Fed funds held at 3.50-3.75% at the June meeting. Net: the hawkish testimony partly offset the dovish CPI surprise. Senate testimony follows Wed 7/15.
