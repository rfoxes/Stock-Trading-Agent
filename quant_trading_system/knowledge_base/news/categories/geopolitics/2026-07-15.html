US–Iran / Strait of Hormuz — third consecutive night of strikes, but oil little-changed on 7/15
CNBC / NPR / Al Jazeera / Fox · 2026-07-15
The US and Iran exchanged strikes for a third straight night. US Central Command ran another ~7-hour wave against Iranian coastal-defense, missile/drone and naval assets near the Strait and along the coast; the US reimposed its naval blockade of Iranian ports and hit a tanker attempting to skirt the blockade. Iran had threatened to block all regional oil-export routes. Oil was little-changed Wednesday despite the escalation — WTI (Aug) settled ~$79.60, Brent (Sep) ~$84.95, holding near one-month highs rather than gapping higher. ~1/5 of world oil + LNG transits Hormuz; slower tanker traffic is the main upside-price risk. Key for the trader: US equities did NOT gap — the tape rose (S&P +0.38%) and VIX fell to 15.67. No >2% equity-futures gap = not halt-worthy, but a live, oil-sensitive tail into the inflation path.
