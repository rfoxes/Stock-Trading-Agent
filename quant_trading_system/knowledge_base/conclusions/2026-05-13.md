## Observations and reasoning

**The 4-day network outage is over.** Broker endpoints
(`/v2/account`, `/v2/positions`, `/v2/orders`) now respond with real data.
Yesterday's escalation in `ATTENTION.md` either prompted an operator fix
or the underlying egress policy changed for unrelated reasons; I can't
distinguish without more context. Either way, the deployment-layer
blocker that ate days 1–4 is no longer the story.

**Two new anomalies took its place, neither of which is mine to fix.**

  1. **Unexplained portfolio.** The paper account has $111,924 of equity
     and 10 long positions totalling ~$208k against ~$96.5k of margin
     debit. Yesterday-me explicitly logged the paper equity baseline as
     $5,000 (via 2026-05-11's `kelly-size` smoke test), and confirmed
     `knowledge_base/journal/` did not exist. Today the journal is still
     empty and the harness has never called `submit`, yet the broker
     holds a real, sized long book that has clearly been there for
     weeks (avg entry prices are well below current marks; +19% on
     GOOGL implies entry was substantially earlier). This matches the
     operating rules' "manual override by the user" scenario verbatim:
     *"Treat as a manual override by the user. Don't close it.
     Document it and ask in the handoff what it is."* I did not close
     a single position and did not tag any of them to a strategy — both
     would be irreversible decisions made on no information. The cost
     of leaving them alone for one more day is small; the cost of
     either flattening someone's intentional portfolio or
     mis-attributing it in the journal is large.

  2. **Bars feed blocked by subscription.** Every bars request returns
     `403 subscription does not permit querying recent SIP data`. This
     was not an issue on prior days only because the proxy was killing
     the request before Alpaca's subscription check could fire; now
     that the proxy is open, we see the subscription-tier wall. The
     CLI has no `--feed` flag, and `market_data_service.get_bars`
     hardcodes the SIP default (the params dict at line ~81 of
     `quant_trading_system/data/market_data_service.py` does not
     include `feed`). Effects: `regime` always returns `unknown`,
     `backtest` and `indicator` are unusable, and `health` /
     `portfolio-health` cannot compute any of the return-based
     metrics the operating rules use to decide rotation. Without
     regime data, calling `set-active` would be guessing, and the
     operating rules call out exactly that: *"Choosing a strategy you
     cannot health-check or backtest is worse than choosing none."*

**Together, those two anomalies make today another correct-by-construction
"do nothing" day.** Even if I were willing to defend a strategy pick on
training-data intuition (I'm not), the buying power is only $15,393 — most
of the equity is already deployed against the unexplained positions, so
placing a meaningfully-sized harness trade would either crowd the manual
book or trigger margin issues. Layering harness trades on top of an
unattributed portfolio would also corrupt the journal: tomorrow's
`portfolio-health` would mix the manual book's P&L with harness P&L and
make every health metric meaningless. The cleanest record is the one
where I don't touch anything until the operator explains the portfolio.

**Why I did *not* edit `active_strategy.md` or any strategy file.** Same
reasoning as days 1–4: no observable regime, no journal history, no
health data. Setting an active strategy today would be a guess against
unverifiable conditions, and the strategy that "matches" the existing
long book (something like `equity_sector_rotation_momentum` or
`equity_trend_following_ema_cross`) would be a post-hoc rationalisation
of someone else's trade, not a decision driven by the harness's own
process.

**Why I did *not* patch `market_data_service.py`.** The fix is small
(`"feed": "iex"` in the params dict) and probably correct, but it's a
production-code change inside a scheduled run with no review and no
journal context for what tests it might break (the regime classifier
in particular is calibrated against whatever feed it was developed
against). The operating rules say to flag and stop on infrastructure
issues, not to fix them inline. Flagged it in today's `ATTENTION.md`
with the exact file/line and the one-line patch suggestion so the
next human pass is trivial.

**Why I did *not* call `log-closed` for any of the 10 positions.**
`log-closed` is for positions that *were* in the journal and have now
closed. None of these positions are in the journal. Logging them as
closed would invent a strategy attribution that doesn't exist.

## Post-run addendum — IEX feed patch (operator-driven, after the scheduled run)

After the scheduled run completed and I wrote the conclusion above, the
operator asked me to patch the bars feed. I edited
`quant_trading_system/data/market_data_service.py` to (a) introduce a
module-level `_DATA_FEED = os.environ.get("ALPACA_DATA_FEED", "iex")`
constant and (b) thread it through both `get_bars`' params and
`get_latest_quote`'s params. End-to-end verification on SPY:

- `bars SPY --days 60` → returned 41 daily bars (2026-03-16 → 2026-05-13),
  prices and volumes look sensible.
- `regime` → `bull, confidence=0.76, sma_200_slope=+0.000692,
  price_vs_sma200=+9.45%, sma_50_vs_200=+1.77%, adx=26.0,
  realized_vol=0.0988, reasoning="Market regime: BULL. Price is 9.4%
  above 200-SMA. ADX at 26 indicates moderate trend."`
- `quote SPY` → still `ask=0.0` after hours (bid=742.16 fine), which is
  an after-hours feed behaviour, not a subscription issue.

This was a deliberate post-run change — not made during the scheduled
trading decision window, and it does not retroactively change today's
no-trade decision (still correct: the unexplained portfolio anomaly
alone is enough to justify standing down). Today's regime number is now
in the record for reference, but the decision was made when `regime`
was still `unknown`.

**Calibration caveat carried into tomorrow's handoff.** The IEX feed
covers a single exchange, so volume figures are a fraction of the
consolidated SIP tape. Several equity strategies use *absolute* volume
thresholds — `equity_breakout_volume_confirmation`, `equity_gap_and_go`,
`equity_opening_range_breakout`, `equity_vwap_reversion`. Their entry
rules will under-trigger on IEX-only volume unless the thresholds are
recalibrated. Strategies that use *relative* volume (current vs. its
own 20-day average) are fine because the normalization is internal to
IEX. Tomorrow's handoff explicitly tells next-Claude to either avoid
absolute-volume strategies until thresholds are recalibrated, or to
recalibrate them deliberately and document the new levels.
