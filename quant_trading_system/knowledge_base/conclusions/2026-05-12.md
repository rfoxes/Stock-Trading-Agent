## Observations and reasoning

**Day 4 of the same outage on the scheduler path.** Counting the four
actual scheduled ticks (2026-05-07, 2026-05-08, 2026-05-11, 2026-05-12; the
2026-05-09/10 weekend is correctly silent), every one has been blocked at
the deployment layer. Days 1–2 were Python-import failures; day 3 the
import side was fixed by the in-tree `_structlog_shim` and the blocker
moved out to the egress proxy; today is bit-for-bit the same proxy
failure as day 3. Nothing in the sandbox configuration has changed
between 2026-05-11 and 2026-05-12.

**The "do nothing" outcome is still the correct one, by both routes.**
The operating rules explicitly elevate inaction to a first-class outcome,
and they specifically warn against modifying strategies or rotating on a
single bad data point. I now have four blocked data points in a row, but
none of them are *market* data — they are *infrastructure* data. Picking
a strategy I cannot health-check, calling `set-active` against an
unreachable broker, or queueing orders against a regime I cannot
classify would all be pure noise on the durable record. The cost of
doing nothing today is zero (no positions open, no money at risk, no
strategy active, the paper account is presumed flat at the $5,000
baseline). The cost of guessing is unbounded.

**Nothing actionable has changed since yesterday.** The diagnostic
delta versus 2026-05-11 is essentially nil:

  - Same CLI behaviour (structlog shim still works, pure-math subcommands
    still run, every network subcommand still 403s).
  - Same on-disk inventory (18 active strategies, 0 archived, no journal,
    no `set-active` call, seed `active_strategy.md`).
  - Same `runs/` directory (still just the day-1 LLM auth artifact).

Given that, I did *not* repeat the broader diagnostic sweep yesterday
did (no second `kelly-size` smoke test, no second `health` lookup,
etc.). Yesterday established that the math-only paths work and the
network-bound paths don't; re-confirming that today would only inflate
the log without changing the decision. I limited today's CLI calls to
the minimum needed to verify the outage is unchanged.

**Operator escalation, repeated and louder.** This is the third
consecutive *trading* day (4 ticks, weekend excluded) where the harness
cannot reach Alpaca. The fix is still one of the three options yesterday
spelled out:

  1. Allowlist `paper-api.alpaca.markets` and `data.alpaca.markets`
     through the sandbox egress proxy.
  2. Rebuild the venv against a Linux-native Python with
     `requirements.txt` installed locally, so `alpaca-py` doesn't have
     to traverse the proxy in the first place. (Note: this alone may
     not fix the outage — `alpaca-py`'s HTTP client still needs egress
     to `*.alpaca.markets`, so option (1) is the more direct fix
     regardless.)
  3. Point the scheduler at a host with working network paths to
     Alpaca.

I am not in a position to do any of these from inside the sandbox, and
neither was day-3-me. Per yesterday's handoff point 4, this is the
escalation: four consecutive blocked scheduled ticks now constitutes a
clear pattern that needs human deployment work, not another patient
day of waiting. I am also taking yesterday's suggestion of writing a
