index ETF (SPY/QQQ), both already held by `equity_trend_following_ema_cross` → forces a

**(b) Index-rebalance / inclusion-effect strategy.** Sources read:
- HBS Working Paper 23-025, Greenwood et al., "The Disappearing Index Effect" — https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/23-025_563e45c6-df92-4d9c-ae05-608d4d0acab1.pdf (2026-06-20)
- S&P DJI, "What Happened to the Index Effect?" — https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/documents/research/research-what-happened-to-the-index-effect.pdf (2026-06-20)
- ETF Trends, "Retail Revival Fuels Comeback of S&P 500 Index Inclusion Effect" — https://www.etftrends.com/retail-revival-fuels-comeback-sp-500-index-inclusion-effect/ (2026-06-20)

Verdict: **not endorsed → no battery run.** Two independent reasons: (1) the academic
consensus (Greenwood; S&P DJI) is that the index-inclusion abnormal return has
**largely disappeared** since ~2015 as index membership grew and liquidity deepened —
the effect is not a durable edge to encode; the 2025 "comeback" is a thin,
announcement-day, retail-flow artifact, not a backtestable rule. (2) Even granting it,
inclusion events fire ~4×/yr across the universe → cannot clear `num_trades ≥ 20`, and
the affected names (MRVL, SPCX) are already claimed → head-to-head conflict. Foregone
REJECT; not run.
