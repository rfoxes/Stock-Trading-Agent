**Root cause (read-only inspection):**
`quant_trading_system/agent_tools.py:1316` calls

constructor as `EquityBacktester(market_data=None)`. The
`strategy_frontmatter` kwarg was removed (or never existed) on the
backtester; the head-to-head wrapper still passes it. One-line fix:
either accept-and-ignore `strategy_frontmatter` in the backtester, or
drop the kwarg in the wrapper and pass frontmatter via `bt.run(...)` if
needed. Outside research-agent scope to fix — same logic as last week's
`StrategyContext.universe` issue.

**Workaround used this run:** ran `cli simulate <strategy> --symbol X`

Ran the archive battery on all 19 strategies. Results:

- 18 KEEP. 12 returned `"strategy has no lifetime journal history;
  archive battery requires trading evidence to evaluate"`. The remaining
  6 returned KEEP for other reasons (trades_rolling > 0 or actions
  within zero-trades window).
- **1 ARCHIVE: `equity_rsi_divergence`** —
  `"no orders or closes in last 60 days (strategy has stopped firing)"`.
  But this strategy was only assigned to HPE on 2026-06-04 (4 days
  ago). It cannot have "stopped firing" — it never had a chance to
  start.

**Root cause analysis (read-only):** The strategy has 2 lifetime journal
events, both `order_rejected` on 2026-06-04: rejected exits on QQQ
($SPY) because the position was already being sold by another strategy
(`held_for_orders` = 28 / 35). These are broker rejections, not real
orders. The archive battery's "no orders or closes in last 60 days"
check counted them as ineligible for trade-count purposes (correctly
finding 0 trades) but as eligible for the lifetime-events safety check
(so it didn't return the "no lifetime history" KEEP branch). Net
effect: 2 phantom events = ARCHIVE verdict on a brand-new strategy.

The manual is explicit: *"Strategies with zero lifetime journal events
return KEEP with a 'no evidence' reason."* Two rejected broker
submissions are arguably zero lifetime trading events. The
implementation's "lifetime_events > 0" check is too lenient — it should
require ≥1 *successful* trade-bearing event (order_submitted that
filled, or trade_closed).

**Did NOT apply the ARCHIVE verdict.** Reasons:
1. It contradicts the documented battery semantics in the manual.
2. Archiving rsi_divergence would orphan HPE — violating the P0
   unclaimed-gate rule the trader enforces every morning.
3. The 2 events that drove the verdict were not trades; they were

## Structural insight worth flagging: addition battery's 20-trade floor is unreachable on single-symbol simulate

The addition battery hard-codes `min_num_trades = 20`. The simulate
backtester runs on a *single symbol* (`--symbol X`). For the equity
library as currently calibrated, *no* strategy reaches 20 trades on a
2-year single-symbol window:

| Strategy | Sample symbol | Trades in 2y |
|---|---|---|
| trend_following_ema_cross | SPY | 1 |
| mean_reversion_bollinger | SPY | 1 |
| momentum_macd_histogram | META | 16 (highest seen) |
| momentum_macd_histogram | AVGO | 15 |
| momentum_macd_histogram | MRVL | 16 |
| breakout_volume_confirmation | MRVL | 8 |
| rsi_divergence | HPE | 3 |
| vwap_reversion | SPY | 0 (skeleton) |
| sector_rotation_momentum | DELL | 0 |
| event_driven_catalyst | AVGO | 0 |

The most active strategy (`momentum_macd_histogram`) tops out at 16
trades on the most active large-cap (META). The 20-trade floor would
require either: (a) multi-symbol simulate (the battery would need to
aggregate trades across the strategy's watchlist), (b) a longer window
(3+ years), or (c) a lower floor calibrated to single-symbol reality
(maybe 10).

This is a structural blocker on adds going forward: even substantively
better candidates will fail the 20-trade floor. Added to "Recent
feedback" in `research_manual.md`.
