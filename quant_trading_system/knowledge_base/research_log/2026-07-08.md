### Decision rule applied (and why it does not violate the 2026-06-16 anti-churn lesson)

The 2026-06-16 manual lesson ("only apply a head-to-head reassignment when BOTH strategies have ≥ ~5 trades;
otherwise record and hold") guards against a **silent** strategy (0 trades) unseating a **real active
trader** by winning the Sharpe=0 ≥ negative tiebreak — *coverage-degrading* churn. **This case is the exact
inverse:** the incumbent is an **un-backtestable, execution-quarantined, non-trading provisional** (0 trades,
Sharpe pinned at 0.0), and the challenger is a real backtested strategy with a **positive baseline-clearing
Sharpe on ≥5 real trades**. Reassigning here swaps a *silent, quarantined placeholder* for an *active,
validated, trading* claim — **coverage-improving**, and precisely what PRIORITY ZERO mandates ("clears
baseline now → becomes a VALIDATED claim… it will start trading on the next weekday run").

Applying the ≥5-trades rule to the *incumbent* would be self-defeating: `event_driven_catalyst` /
`pairs_cointegration` are un-backtestable and ALWAYS have 0 trades, so it would make it **impossible to ever
convert any `event_catalyst`/`pairs_arbitrage` provisional**, guaranteeing permanent drift — the very failure
the doctrine exists to prevent. So the guard is correctly applied to the **challenger's** trade count (it
must have a real, non-fluke Sharpe): **convert when challenger clears 0.5 AND has ≥5 trades**; hold when the
challenger clears 0.5 but on <5 trades (a 3–4 trade Sharpe is fluke-prone per the same lesson).

### HARNESS GAP found (flagged for operator — Open Q#8)

A **cross-strategy** validated claim via `triage-symbol` does NOT clear a pre-existing provisional marker.
The `verdict:"claimed"` path in `agent_tools.triage_symbol` calls `memory.clear_library_gap(sym)` but not
`memory.clear_provisional_claim(sym)`. So when strategy B validly claims a symbol whose provisional marker
was keyed to strategy A, the marker goes stale and the symbol **stays execution-quarantined despite being a
validated claim** — silently defeating the conversion. I worked around it by calling `clear_provisional_claim`
directly (the manual explicitly authorizes this: "if you claimed via a different path, call the equivalent of
`clear_provisional_claim`"). Fix: the claim path should clear any provisional marker for `sym` regardless of
which strategy previously held it.
